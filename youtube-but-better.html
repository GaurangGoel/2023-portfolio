Product Redesign

Youtube but better!

  • Timeline
    3 weeks (Fall 2022)
  • Role
    Product Designer
  • Tools Used
    Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator
Made with

Overview

I am always eager to improve and innovate existing platforms to create better experiences for users. With the ever-growing popularity of YouTube, I saw an opportunity to redesign its interface for both desktop and mobile platforms, aiming to offer a more streamlined, clear, and intuitive user experience.

As a fun fact, I completed this project before YouTube's official redesign launch in November 2022. I had a sneak peek into the future of YouTube and my redesign was ahead of the curve.

Research & Analysis

  • User Interviews: I conducted one-on-one interviews with 10 YouTube users to gain in-depth insights into their usage patterns, preferences, and frustrations. This qualitative research helped me to understand the users' needs and expectations.

  • Surveys: I created an online survey and gathered responses from 100 YouTube users, asking about their experiences and opinions on the current design. The survey provided valuable quantitative data that informed the redesign process.

  • SWOT Analysis: To better understand YouTube's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats, I conducted a SWOT analysis that helped me to identify areas where the platform could be improved and to prioritize design changes.

  • Information Architecture Analysis: I studied the current organization of content and navigation on YouTube to identify areas of confusion or inefficiency. This analysis informed the restructuring of content and the design of a more intuitive navigation system.

Pain Points

"The homepage feels cluttered and disorganized, while the Shorts page appears sparse and empty. It's challenging to navigate."


"Sometimes, it's hard to find song lyrics on YouTube, and I end up searching on Google. Having a lyrics mode would be a great addition."


"The desktop Shorts mode feels awkward. Occasionally, it looks just like a regular video, which can be confusing."


"I often find the homepage recommendations irrelevant. There's definitely room for improvement in the suggestion algorithm."

Problem Statement

Current YouTube users experience a cluttered interface, difficulty in discovering and navigating content, and suboptimal video playback controls. These issues hinder user engagement and overall satisfaction with the platform. The redesign project aims to enhance the user experience by simplifying the interface, improving content organization, increasing user engagement, and facilitating seamless navigation on both desktop and mobile versions.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

After sketching out my initial ideas, I moved on to create more detailed wireframes for both desktop and mobile versions. The wireframes allowed me to refine the layout, content organization, and navigation before moving on to high-fidelity prototyping.

Home & Search

The redesigned Home and Search pages offer users an enhanced experience, with a focus on content discovery and personalized recommendations. It now features a cleaner, more streamlined layout that emphasizes the most relevant content for each user.

The Search page not only serves as a tool for users to find specific content but also as an opportunity to discover something new.

Channel & Shorts

The Shorts page now fits the desktop version much better, providing a seamless browsing experience for users interested in short-form content.

The Channel page now offers greater customization options for creators, enabling them to express their unique brand and showcase their content in a more personalized way.

All-New Karaoke Mode

As part of the redesigned YouTube experience, I am excited to unveil the all-new Karaoke Mode. This innovative feature caters to the needs of music enthusiasts and aspiring singers alike by transforming the platform into an interactive and engaging karaoke hub.

All-New Action Bar for Mobile

The redesigned YouTube mobile experience features a versatile and user-friendly action bar that significantly enhances the navigation and overall usability of the platform. This action bar, located at the bottom of the screen, provides quick and easy access to essential features such as Home, Explore, Subscriptions, Library, and Notifications.

Key Learnings

  • User Research is Crucial: The initial user interviews, surveys, SWOT analysis, and competitive analysis were instrumental in identifying pain points and understanding user needs.

  • Adaptability is Key: Understanding the differences between desktop and mobile platforms and their unique constraints led to a more adaptable design that catered to the specific needs of each platform.

  • Simplicity and Clarity: A clean and uncluttered interface is vital for improving user engagement and satisfaction. By focusing on the most important elements and using whitespace effectively, the redesigned YouTube became more accessible and enjoyable to use.
