Feature Design

CarView: A Hackathon Project

  • Timeline
    24 Hours
  • Role
    Product Designer
  • Tools Used
    Figma, Blender, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator
  • Prototype
    Check it out!
Made with

Overview

Our team set out to tackle Toyota's end-to-end vehicle experience challenge during a 24-hour hackathon. Our goal was to create an innovative and user-friendly interaction between drivers and their car windows using Augmented Reality (AR). We aimed to enhance the driving experience by adding a touch of personalization and excitement to an everyday vehicle element. I'm proud to announce that our project won 3rd place in the Toyota challenge!

Discover our innovation on DevPost!

Challenge

We focused on Toyota's challenge to improve the end-to-end vehicle experience by developing a solution that enhances the relationship between drivers and their car windows. Our creative approach involved using AR technology to enable drivers to interact with their car windows in a more intuitive and engaging manner.

Strategy

To ensure the success of our project, we established a clear roadmap and designated roles for each team member. Our well-structured approach enabled us to complete the project within the tight 24-hour timeframe. We also conducted a thorough research process, gathering feedback from potential users to inform our design decisions.

We not only finished within 24 hours, but we also had time to spare for a victory dance party (which we totally crushed, by the way).

Brainstorming and User Research

Our brainstorming sessions generated numerous ideas for car window interactions. To validate our concepts, we conducted interviews with 14 people on the streets, asking for their opinions on the desired controls and interactions for a car window. This feedback helped us narrow down our ideas and provided valuable insights for our design process.

High-Fidelity Design

Using the insights gathered from our user research, we created a high-fidelity design that combined both functionality and aesthetics. This design aimed to enhance the driving experience while also catering to user preferences and maintaining a visually appealing interface.

Implementation and AR Integration

After finalizing our design, we brought the Toyota Supra to life in 3D and integrated our innovative window design using AR technology. This combination transformed the car window into an interactive and personalized element of the vehicle, showcasing the potential of AR in automotive design.

Key Learnings

  1. Driving Innovation: The importance of creative problem-solving in the development of unique car window interactions.
  2. Time Management: Balancing creativity and efficiency within a 24-hour timeframe.
  3. Accessibility and Aesthetics: The significance of combining both form and function in design.
  4. Adapting to Challenges: Learning to overcome unexpected obstacles, such as indoor football incidents and broken laptops.

By tackling Toyota's end-to-end vehicle experience challenge, our team successfully developed an innovative and engaging solution that enhances the relationship between drivers and their car windows using AR technology. This project not only demonstrated our ability to work effectively within tight deadlines but also showcased the potential of AR in revolutionizing automotive design and user experiences.
